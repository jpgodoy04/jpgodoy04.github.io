Iconic Landmarks & Cities

Shibuya

Famous for its enormous pedestrian crossing, hundreds of fashion stores, and neon signs. Shibuya is the center of Tokyo's cutting-edge youth culture.

Mt Fuji

Mount Fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan and a stratovolcano. The present-day mountain is a composite of three successive volcanoes.

Tokyo

It's Japan's top spot for contemporary art, pop culture, shopping, and entertainment. The city itself is what enchants visitors the most.

Historic & Spiritual Hubs

Kyoto

Kyoto is considered the cultural capital of Japan. It is home to numerous Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, palaces and gardens, many designated as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Famous for its thousands of vibrant red torii gates, this Shinto shrine in Kyoto is dedicated to Inari, the god of rice. Visitors walk through the mesmerizing tunnels of gates that wind up a mountain.

Natural Beauty

Cherry Blossoms (Sakura)

Sakura is the iconic Japanese cherry blossom, which blooms in spring. This beautiful, fleeting flower is celebrated with 'hanami' (flower viewing) parties, where people gather under the trees to eat, drink, and appreciate the transient beauty of nature.

Art & Cuisine

Anime

A distinct form of animation originating in Japan, characterized by ⁢colorful artwork, vibrant characters, and fantastical themes.

Japanese Cuisines

Incredibly diverse, emphasizing seasonality and quality of ingredients. Raw seafood is one of the most famous dishes, often eaten as sashimi.

Ramen

A beloved noodle soup with Chinese-style wheat noodles in a rich broth, flavored with soy sauce or miso and various toppings.
